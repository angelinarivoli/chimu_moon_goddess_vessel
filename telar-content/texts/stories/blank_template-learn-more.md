The Chimu were successful at conquest because they incorporated local nobility into their ranks and built their religious traditions to incorporate those of the earlier local civilization, the Moche's, including the Staff God. Another contributor to their successful expansion was that the Chimu had an extensive irrigation system, controlling the Moche, Chicama, and Viru valleys. The Chimu canal system irrigated 201.4 square kilometers of land, more than present day. Because of their location close to the Pacific Coast, iconography from the ocean is prevalent in their ceramics and murals.
