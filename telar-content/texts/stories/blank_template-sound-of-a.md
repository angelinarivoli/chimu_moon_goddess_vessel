title: "Sound of a Chimu Whistle"
---

  One of the most fascinating aspects of the Chimu whistle are the hidden interior structures. This diagram is a representation of how the Chimu whistle  is engineered  and shows the hidden whistle component.  X-ray images provided evidence that the interiors of andean whistling vessels from this period typically contain multiple hollow chambers connected by small passages. There is a functioning chamber and a chamber that contains the whistle mechanisms. The chambers work together to create the sound when air or water moves through the object.  Because all the hidden chambers are enclosed within the ceramic wall before firing, the craftsman had to carefully plan the vessel's construction. In many of the whistles the hidden whistle component is found inside the head of the bird or decorative figures represented.  The complexity of these hidden chambers demonstrate the Chimu's technical skill. The Chimu artisans combined artistic expression and creative engineering to create the whistle.
