  The large variation of marine iconography throughout Chimu Goddess blackware is symbolic of the multitude of ways the ocean provided sustenance to the Chimu. Early creation myths frequently reference the ocean as a point of origin for the Chimu society. A significant resource, the ocean provided the climate necessary to support an abundant population of shellfish, seabird, fish, and sea mammal life that fed the Chimu. Additionally, the sea supported Chimu agriculture by providing a large water source which was a critical factor in the construction of their complex crop irrigation systems. As the Chimu society expanded, the currents of the ocean would continue to play a significant role in military conquest and trading routes. Sailing atop a specially designed balsa raft, fishermen and soldiers relied on the ocean as an efficient travel method between endeavors. In order to ensure a continuous supply of marine life, water, and safety for soldiers and fishermen alike, the Chimu religion personified the ocean in the form of several deities such as the Chimu Goddess and the Moon Animal seen on the body of the ceramic above. Once personified, three dimensional representations of the Chimu Goddess across stirrup spout blackware vessels allowed individuals to engage in worship of the goddess through ritual, ensuring their personal wellbeing and the wellbeing of the Chimu society at large.
